FAQ
Questions
Does normal copy/paste still work? ▼
Completely unchanged. Dart only activates when you press a slot key within half a second after ⌘+C or ⌘+V. If you don't, it's just normal copy and paste. Zero interference.
Why D, S, F instead of 1, 2, 3? ▼
They're right next to the C key — so after ⌘+C your fingers are already there. Faster muscle memory. But you can switch to 1-2-3 or assign any keys you want in Settings.
Why does it need Accessibility permission? ▼
Dart captures keyboard shortcuts system-wide — that's how it knows you pressed ⌘+C followed by D. macOS requires Accessibility permission for this. It's used exclusively for hotkey detection. No screen reading, no keystroke logging, no other purpose.
Is my clipboard data sent anywhere? ▼
No. Everything stays on your Mac in local app storage. No cloud, no servers, no analytics. We literally cannot see what you copy.
Why only 3 slots? ▼
Three is the sweet spot — you can remember what's in D, S, and F without thinking. More slots are unlockable as you use the app (up to 9). But 3 handles the vast majority of real-world use cases. Speed over storage.
Is it a subscription? ▼
No. $4.99 once. You own it. No recurring charges, no premium tier, no "upgrade to unlock" games. One payment, forever.
What about other clipboard managers? ▼
Most clipboard managers record everything and make you search later. Dart takes a different approach: intentional storage with instant recall. You decide what goes where. No popup menus, no scrolling through history to find what you need. Just keyboard shortcuts and muscle memory.
