How It Works

Three Moves. That's It.

1

Copy Something

Select text, image, whatever — press ⌘+C like normal. Dart shows a brief HUD: it's listening for a slot key.

⌘ C → D

If you don't press a slot key, it's just a normal copy. Nothing changes.

2

Paste From Any Slot

Press ⌘+V then immediately press the slot key. The saved content pastes — even if you've copied other things since.

⌘ V → D

Works in any app — browsers, editors, terminals, email, everywhere.

3

Browse Your History

Click the Dart icon in your menu bar to see all three slots and your full clipboard history. Search, assign items to slots, or copy directly.

Menu bar shows slot contents at a glance. Keyboard-first, but the UI is always there.

FAQ

Questions

Does normal copy/paste still work?▼
Yes — completely unchanged. ⌘+C copies to your clipboard like always. Dart only activates when you press a slot key (D, S, or F) within half a second after copying or pasting. If you don't press a slot key, nothing different happens.
Can I change the slot keys?▼
Yes. D, S, F are the defaults because they're close to the C key for fast muscle memory. You can switch to Classic mode (1, 2, 3) or assign any key you want in Settings.
Why does Dart need Accessibility permission?▼
Dart captures keyboard shortcuts system-wide — that's how it knows you pressed ⌘+C followed by D. macOS requires Accessibility permission for this. Dart uses it exclusively for hotkey detection and nothing else. No screen reading, no keystroke logging.
Is my clipboard data sent anywhere?▼
No. Everything stays on your Mac in local app storage. No cloud, no servers, no analytics, no telemetry. We literally cannot see what you copy.
Does it work with images and files?▼
Dart v1 supports text content. Image and file support is coming in a future update based on user feedback.
Why only 3 slots? Why not 10?▼
Three is the sweet spot for muscle memory — you can remember what's in D, S, and F without thinking. More slots are unlockable as you use the app. But honestly, 3 handles 90% of use cases. We designed for speed, not storage.
Is it a subscription?▼
No. $4.99 one-time. You own it forever. No recurring charges, no premium tier, no "upgrade to unlock" games.
